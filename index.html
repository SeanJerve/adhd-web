The ADHD Assessment Process

Understanding the path to a formal diagnosis is the first crucial step. We break down both NHS and private routes.

NHS Pathway

The NHS route typically involves seeing your GP first for a referral to a local specialist mental health service (known as secondary care).

  • Step 1: Initial GP consultation & screening.
  • Step 2: Referral to specialist team (long waiting times can occur).
  • Step 3: Full diagnostic assessment with a psychiatrist or specialist.
  • Key Concept: Right to Choose (if available in your region) allows referral to a specialist provider.

Private Assessment

Private care offers faster access to assessment but requires self-funding. Ensure the clinician is a UK-registered psychiatrist experienced in ADHD diagnosis.

  • Cost: Varies widely, usually £1,000 - £2,500.
  • Process: Direct booking with a specialist (psychiatrist or neurodevelopmental specialist).
  • Post-Diagnosis: Crucial for setting up a Shared Care Agreement with your NHS GP for ongoing treatment.

What to Expect at an Appointment

Assessments are often lengthy (1-3 hours) and involve gathering information about your current symptoms, developmental history (childhood), and how ADHD affects major life areas (work, relationships, etc.).

You may be asked to bring school reports or old documents for corroboration. The goal is to build a complete picture of your life, not just a snapshot of your current state.

Key Topics & Materials

  • Current symptoms & challenges.
  • Childhood development & school experiences.
  • Family history (if known).
  • Impact on work, study, and relationships.
  • (Optional) Old school reports or performance reviews.

Diagnosis & Treatment Options

Medication Options

Medication is a primary treatment. The two main types are stimulants (e.g., Methylphenidate, Lisdexamfetamine) and non-stimulants (e.g., Atomoxetine). This process, known as 'titration', is carefully managed by a specialist to find the right dose.

Key Points:

  • NICE guidelines recommend medication as a first-line treatment for adults.
  • Stimulants are fast-acting; non-stimulants take several weeks.
  • Regular monitoring (blood pressure, heart rate) is required.

Non-Medication Treatment

Therapy and coaching are highly effective, often used in combination with medication. These approaches focus on building skills and understanding the impact of ADHD on your life.

Key Approaches:

  • CBT for ADHD: Adapts Cognitive Behavioural Therapy to target executive function challenges.
  • ADHD Coaching: Practical, goal-oriented support for daily organisation and productivity.
  • Psychoeducation: Learning about ADHD to "de-mystify" your symptoms and reduce shame.

Understanding Shared Care Agreements (SCAs)

If you are diagnosed privately, an SCA is a vital document. It's an agreement between your private specialist and your NHS GP, allowing your GP to take over prescribing your medication on the NHS. This saves you significant ongoing costs. Gaining GP agreement *before* starting private treatment is highly recommended.

About ADHD Assessor

ADHD Assessor is an independent educational platform created by UK healthcare professionals to improve understanding of ADHD and the complex assessment process. Our goal is to empower individuals and families with accurate, digestible information to navigate their journey. We champion evidence-based care and informed decision-making.

Important Disclaimer

This site provides general information and does not replace professional medical advice. Always consult a qualified professional (your GP or a psychiatrist) about your personal situation, diagnosis, and treatment plan. We are not a medical clinic and cannot provide individual clinical advice.
